Author: Amanda Langell

Lionel Messi Reaches New International Milestone in Argentina's Victory Over Angola

In Argentina’s 2–0 win against Angola at the Estádio 11 de Novembro, Lionel Messi recorded both a goal and an assist, marking another milestone in his international career. The assist, delivered to Lautaro Martínez in the 43rd minute, represents Messi’s ninth career assist to the Inter striker, matching his record for the most assists to a single teammate, a mark he previously reached with Gonzalo Higuaín and Sergio Agüero (Langell).

Just one more assist to Martínez would set Messi apart, adding another record to his illustrious career, which already includes consecutive Copa América titles and the 2022 FIFA World Cup victory (Langell).

Messi, who has now scored 115 goals in 187 appearances for Argentina, will need to wait until the new year to attempt to surpass his personal record for assists, after Argentina’s friendly with Australia scheduled for November 17 was postponed. Despite only appearing once during the November international window, Messi’s performance was historic: he both assisted Martínez and scored his 115th international goal in the 82nd minute (Langell).

Following the international break, Messi will shift focus to Inter Miami as the club aims for its first MLS Cup. Inter Miami advanced past Nashville SC in the opening round of the 2025 MLS Cup playoffs and will face FC Cincinnati in the Eastern Conference semifinals on November 23. With 20 goal contributions in his last eight matches across club and country, Messi is expected to play a pivotal role in leading Inter Miami to their first-ever conference final and potentially earning back-to-back MLS MVP honors (Langell).

Fun Facts About Soccer

Author: Shervin Babaei

10 Fun and Surprising Facts About Soccer

Soccer is one of the most popular sport in the world, and there are plenty of cool facts about that, that most plyaers and fans might not known of. Even though the rules look simple, the history and details behind the game are full of interesting surprises. From record-breaking players to unusual traditions, soccer has grown into a sport that brings millions of people together. Here are ten fun facts that show just how incredible the game really is.

One of the craziest facts about soccer is how massive the FIFA World Cup actually was. There were more than 3 billion people who watched the 2022 World Cup — that’s almost half of everyone on the planet! The final between Argentina and France alone had over a billion viewers. This makes the World Cup the single most-watched sporting event in the whole world, even bigger than the Olympics.

Another fun fact is that soccer fields aren’t all exactly the same size. The rules allow fields to be anywhere between 100–130 yards long and 50–100 yards wide. That means some teams can play on much larger or smaller fields than others, depending on their stadium. Coaches even choose strategies based on the size of the field, which can make a big difference in how the game feels.

Some players have also made unforgettable records. For example, Cristiano Ronaldo is the first male player to score over 120 international goals, while Lionel Messi has won the most Ballon d'Or awards, with eight wins. These awards are given to the best player in the world each year. Watching these two legends play at the same time is something future generations might never see again.

Soccer even has some weird rules and traditions. For example, the referee’s whistle was not always used. Before whistles were invented, referees raised a handkerchief to stop the game! Another unusual rule is that a goalkeeper can be given a yellow card for wasting too much time! rules like these keep the game fair and fast-paced.

Finally, soccer balls haven’t always looked the way they do now. The classic black-and-white pattern was designed in 1970 so the ball would be easier to see on black-and-white TV broadcasts. Today, balls are made with advanced materials that help them fly straighter, reduce the weight and go even faster. Modern designs are tested using wind tunnels, computer simulations, and even some robots that kick the ball with perfect accuracy.

Best young players in the world 2025

Author: Alex Harris, Ned Holmes

A new wave of young football stars is reshaping the game in 2025. These players may be under 23, but they’re already delivering world-class performances for club and country.

Young football players continue to capture the excitement of fans around the world, especially as more athletes break into top-level teams at earlier ages. As the 2025 season unfolds, the global football landscape is filled with gifted prospects who combine technical ability, maturity, and standout performances despite being under the age of 23. Analysts have taken note of how this new generation is adapting to the physical and tactical demands of modern football, bringing fresh energy and creativity to their clubs and national teams (Harris & Holmes, 2025).

Among the rising stars, several young players have already made significant impacts in Europe’s biggest leagues. Talents such as Désiré Doué at PSG and Estevão at Chelsea are demonstrating exceptional skill, with Doué showcasing explosive pace and Estevão standing out for his fearless, direct style of play. Their early success highlights how top clubs continue investing heavily in youth, trusting young players with major roles far earlier than in previous eras (Harris & Holmes, 2025).

At the top of the rankings are players such as João Neves, Jude Bellingham, and Pedri—youngsters who have already reached world-class status. Neves has impressed with his defensive intelligence and ball-carrying ability at PSG, Bellingham continues to dominate midfield for Real Madrid and England, and Pedri remains one of Barcelona’s most influential playmakers. These players have set new standards for what is expected from young professionals competing at the elite level (Harris & Holmes, 2025).

Leading the list is Barcelona’s Lamine Yamal, widely regarded as the best young footballer in the world in 2025. At only 18 years old, Yamal has already become a crucial attacking force for both Barcelona and Spain, drawing comparisons to Lionel Messi due to his creativity, maturity, and ability to perform under pressure. His rapid rise reflects the extraordinary quality of this new footballing generation and suggests that the next decade of world football will be defined by these emerging stars (Harris & Holmes, 2025).

Soccer Tech & Analytics: How Clubs Are Using Data to Find the Next Star

Author: AnalyiSport

Who Are Football’s Star Data Analysts?

Many people know famous football players like Mo Salah, Cristiano Ronaldo, Messi or great coaches like Jürgen Klopp. But not many know about the data analysts who help teams win from behind the scenes. These people study numbers, videos, and player movements to help coaches make smart decisions. Even though they don’t play on the field, their work has become very important for modern football (AnalyiSport, 2022).

Football clubs today collect a huge amount of information every time players train or play a match. Cameras and GPS trackers record how fast players run, where they move, when they pass, shoot, or tackle. In fact, experts say that more than 2,000 pieces of data are recorded in every match, plus 1.5 million tracking points about player movements. That’s a massive amount of information! Data analysts study all of this to help teams improve and make better choices (AnalyiSport, 2022).

One of the best-known data experts is Ian Graham, who leads the data department at Liverpool FC. He first studied physics, not football, and even earned a PhD. Later, he started working on football statistics and eventually joined Liverpool in 2012. His team has helped the club sign great players and build strong tactics using smart data analysis. Some people even call Liverpool’s method “Merseyball,” based on their creative use of numbers and science (AnalyiSport, 2022).

Leading the list is Barcelona’s Lamine Yamal, widely regarded as the best young footballer in the world in 2025. At only 18 years old, Yamal has already become a crucial attacking force for both Barcelona and Spain, drawing comparisons to Lionel Messi due to his creativity, maturity, and ability to perform under pressure. His rapid rise reflects the extraordinary quality of this new footballing generation and suggests that the next decade of world football will be defined by these emerging stars (Harris & Holmes, 2025).

Graham and his team use special formulas to understand what actions really help the team score goals. One of these ideas is called “goal probability added.” This means they check how much each player’s move—like a pass or shot—increases the chance of scoring. For example, a risky pass that leads to a chance is more valuable than a safe pass that doesn’t help the team attack. This helps teams find underrated players who are better than they look at first glance (AnalyiSport, 2022).

Liverpool isn’t the only team investing in data experts. Manchester City also hires top scientists, including people who worked at places like Harvard University or even for the government. These new analysts study advanced math, statistics, and computer science to help their team stay ahead. Some of them come from totally different jobs—like teaching high school physics or designing video games—showing that anyone with the right skills can make a difference in football (AnalyiSport, 2022).

Experts believe that data analysts will become even more important in the future. Some say that soon these specialists might even stand on the sidelines during matches with laptops, helping coaches make decisions in real time. Whether that happens or not, one thing is clear: data analysis is changing football, and the people behind the numbers are becoming stars in their own way (AnalyiSport, 2022).
